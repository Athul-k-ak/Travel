About Kerala

In the heart of nature

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a tropical paradise known for its lush greenery and serene backwaters. The state boasts a rich cultural heritage, with vibrant festivals, classical dance forms, and traditional Ayurvedic practices. Its diverse landscapes range from tranquil beaches and scenic hill stations to wildlife-rich forests. Kerala is also famous for its delicious cuisine, featuring a unique blend of spices and flavors.

All Districts

A Journey Through Its Vibrant Districts

Kasaragod: The northernmost district, Kasaragod is famous for its rich cultural heritage, historical forts, and beautiful beaches.

Kannur: Known for its vibrant Theyyam performances and beautiful beaches, Kannur offers a blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Wayanad: A scenic district with lush forests and picturesque waterfalls, Wayanad is ideal for trekking and eco-tourism.

Kozhikode: Famous for its historic spice trade, Kozhikode is renowned for its delicious cuisine and scenic backwaters.

Malappuram: Rich in history and natural beauty, Malappuram is known for its ancient temples and scenic landscapes.

Thrissur: Often called the cultural capital of Kerala, Thrissur is celebrated for its grand temple festivals and rich history.

Ernakulam: The commercial hub of Kerala, Ernakulam is celebrated for its modernity, bustling markets, and proximity to the picturesque backwaters.

Kottayam: Renowned for its picturesque backwaters and as the center of Kerala's publishing industry, Kottayam is a serene and culturally rich district.

Pathanamthitta: Often referred to as the Pilgrim's Land, Pathanamthitta is known for its numerous temples and lush natural beauty.

Alappuzha: Often called the Venice of the East, Alappuzha is famous for its enchanting backwaters and houseboat cruises.

Idukki: Known for its stunning hill stations and wildlife sanctuaries, Idukki is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Palakkad: Often referred to as the Gateway to Kerala, Palakkad is famous for its lush paddy fields and historical forts.

Kollam: Known for its historical significance in the spice trade and its scenic backwaters, Kollam is a charming destination with serene landscapes.

Thiruvananthapuram: The state capital, Thiruvananthapuram is known for its historical temples, vibrant arts scene, and beautiful beaches like Kovalam.
